Methodology

HOW WE FIND THE REAL NUMBER

Every vertical uses the same four-stage normalization pipeline, adapted to its specific data sources and manipulation patterns.

01

Ingest

We pull published ratings, review text, timestamps, reviewer histories, posted wait times, license databases, and complaint records across every major platform — simultaneously and continuously.

02

Detect

AI-powered signals analyze velocity patterns, linguistic fingerprints, reviewer network clustering, geographic anomalies, license verification gaps, and AI-generated text markers. 37+ active signals across verticals.

03

Normalize

Suspicious reviews are down-weighted. Wait time inflation factors are calculated against live API data. Contractor credentials are cross-verified. Cross-platform divergence is scored and explained.

04

Output

A normalized score — Inflation Factor for parks, Value Rating for restaurants, Trust Score for services — the number that should have been there all along. Every output includes a confidence band and distortion type label.

FAQ

COMMON QUESTIONS

What is RatingValue.org?

RatingValue.org is a rating intelligence company that builds vertical-specific consumer platforms exposing rating manipulation. We operate WaitTimeTruth.com for theme park wait time inflation tracking, RestaurantRating.org for restaurant review fraud detection, and partner with AmusementRating.org for crowdsourced theme park operations auditing. Each platform uses AI-powered signal detection to normalize ratings and show consumers what scores really mean. RatingValue is the methodology and API layer that connects all three verticals.

How does rating normalization work?

Rating normalization compares published ratings or posted metrics against independently verified data to produce a corrected score. For theme parks, we compare posted wait times against actual guest-reported waits to calculate an Inflation Factor. For restaurants, we analyze review patterns across Google, Yelp, and TripAdvisor to detect fake reviews, AI-generated text, review velocity spikes, and reviewer network clustering — then produce a normalized Value Rating. For home services, we cross-reference published ratings with license verification, BBB complaints, and review authenticity signals to generate a Trust Score.

Are online ratings trustworthy?

Research suggests approximately 30% of online reviews are fake or manipulated. Consumer trust in online reviews has declined from 76% in 2019 to approximately 46% today. Theme parks routinely overstate posted wait times by 20–80% to drive skip-the-line purchases. Home service contractors are the most review-manipulated vertical on Google. RatingValue.org exists to bridge this trust gap by providing normalized, signal-validated ratings across the verticals where manipulation is most prevalent and consumer harm is greatest.

Who is behind RatingValue?

RatingValue.org is operated by Amusement Standards LLC, a risk intelligence company based in Asbury Park, New Jersey. The company was founded by Adam Brousell, who brings 20+ years of amusement park operations experience. Amusement Standards also operates the AS 360 platform, a B2B risk intelligence system for the attractions industry. RatingValue is the consumer-facing arm — the platforms that generate crowdsourced data and make it publicly accessible.

Is there an API?

A RatingValue API is planned for businesses, insurers, and researchers who need access to normalized rating data at scale. The API will provide Inflation Factors for theme parks, Value Ratings for restaurants, and Trust Scores for home services — all with confidence bands and distortion type labels. Early access is available for enterprise partners. Contact us for details.
